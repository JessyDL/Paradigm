the principal differences between this method and utility::geometry::create_quad() is that the plane can be subdivided, and has finer control over the UV's, making it better suited for 3D geometry.

Parameters
[in,out]cachethe cache to store the generated geometry information on.
[in]sizethe extent in the XZ axis.
[in]subdivisionsthe amount of times to subdivide the mesh in the XZ axis.
[in]uvScalemultiplier on the UV scale, where {1, 1} is the normalized coordinates.
Returns
the handle to the generated geometry data.
Note
the final size of the object is always 2x the extents. This means that a size {2, 1} results into a min-coordinate {-2, -1} and max-coordinate {2, 1}.
